 How We Work

The Mold Remediation Process

  • Initial Assessment: Upon arrival, our specialists will listen to your concerns and conduct a hazard assessment followed by a thorough examination of your property to determine the source of the moisture-caused mold and the extent of the contamination. This step is vital for developing a strategic plan of action that addresses your specific situation.
  • Containment & Engineering Controls: Utilizing state-of-the-art equipment, we set up a negative pressure containment along with HEPA-filtered air cleaners and Personal Protective Equipment and physically remove all mold contamination, including disassembling wall and floor coverings, etc., to thoroughly remediate the mold.
  • Cleaning & Treating: Mold contamination easily spreads to other areas through airborne spores that are released by all molds. These contaminants can pose health risks to you and your family. Our team thoroughly cleans the affected areas, including everything in the contained area, including the containment walls and the equipment used before it is removed from the containment.
  • Drying: After the mold remediation work is completed, we thoroughly dry any remaining wet or damp materials, such as wall coverings and structural framing materials, to ensure mold will not regrow.
  • Documentation: Every step taken and every piece of equipment is continuously monitored for performance, moisture content, and psychrometric measurements are taken and recorded. Photos of the conditions and the loss are taken, and drying logs, communications, and client requests are documented.
  • Insurance Assistance: We prioritize the property owner's needs and concerns, ensuring their property is clean, dry, and ready to rebuild efficiently to pre-loss condition. After addressing the immediate needs, AmeriClean assists you with navigating insurance claims and providing documentation and support to help property owners get the proper coverage.

 Prevention Methods

DIY Tips and Preventative Measures for Mold Contamination

While professional help is essential in addressing mold contamination, here are some DIY tips and preventative measures you can take to avoid mold contamination:

Call Now Get a Quote

  • Ensure Good Ventilation

    Keep air circulating to reduce indoor moisture.

  • Regularly Inspect Inside & Out

    Frequently inspect and maintain all plumbing supplies and drains. Replace any deteriorating caulking and weather stripping. Repair and thoroughly dry any indoor leaks… whether from condensation on windows, leaking plumbing systems, or from outdoors to the inside, keep the property dry.

  • Be Aware

    High humidity levels can also cause mold and structural damage. Watch for wet or damp areas, clean up all water spills, and ventilate steamy rooms such as showers and kitchens. Don’t stack cardboard boxes against cold exterior walls. Be a fresh-air freak and ventilate!

  • Water Detection Devices

    Install inexpensive water detection devices near potential water sources to alert you of leaks as they happen. Protect out-of-sight areas such as behind toilets, laundry machines, under sinks, and on the floor near your water heater.

  • Proper Landscaping

    Make sure your yard slopes away from your foundation along with downspouts and gutter extensions so rainwater and snow melt does not come into your home. Use covers on window wells.

In-Depth Techniques in Mold Remediation

AmeriClean strictly adheres to the ANSI Certified International Standard for Professional Mold Remediation (ANSI/IICRC S520). This standard calls for erecting engineering controls including negative pressure containments, HEPA-filtered air purifiers and equipment, and site-specific protocols designed to protect the workers and occupants from contamination as well as protecting the rest of the structure from cross-contamination.

Remediation work is performed in a negative pressure containment. The air within containment is continuously being “scrubbed” with large HEPA air filtration devices. All of our vacuuming and sanding equipment is HEPA filtered, and every surface is carefully HEPA bristle vacuumed, then HEPA sanded, then vacuumed again. Then, all horizontal surfaces are thoroughly damp, wiped, and dried.

Health Considerations of Mold Remediation

Mold contamination can have significant health impacts. Depending on the health profile of the individual, the amount of mold, the exposure time, and the type of mold means there are thousands of combinations that have the potential to negatively affect human health and well-being. All types of mold are allergenic, and some types produce toxins and even be infectious.
